About UofT STAR

Who we are

We are the University of Toronto Student Team for Amateur Rocketry (UofT STAR / UTSTAR). Our mission is to design, test, and launch innovative model rockets while training a diverse community of builders in avionics, structures, propulsion, and flight systems.

President’s Message

Hi there! I am ________, the President and Founder of the U of T Student Team for Amateur Rocketry (UofT STAR). As a second‑year Math and Physics double‑major with a serious interest in spaceflight and model rocketry, I decided to take my hobby one notch higher by assembling a team of students who share my passion. I am thrilled to have an executive committee of eight students who are competent and committed to achieving our project goals.

UofT STAR is the only rocketry team run by students of the Faculty of Arts & Science (FAS). Our vision is to become the go‑to rocketry team for FAS students and, eventually, the wider university. Students who want hands‑on experience in CAD and solid modeling, fluid dynamics, propulsion, and avionics will find our team a great fit—without compromising academic commitments.

Our mission is to design, test, and launch mid‑ to large‑scale model rockets, each showcasing a unique technology. Our first project aims to demonstrate a model rocket that can propulsively land without a parachute. We welcome all experience levels. We’d love to have you on board!

What we do

  • Design reviews at SRR → PDR → CDR → FRR.
  • Avionics & software in C/C++ and Python; simulation & HIL testing.
  • Structures & propulsion: composites, thrust characterization, recovery/VTOL.
  • Flight operations with documented procedures and safety training.
